Moulay Idriss zerhoun, the birthplace of a dynasty

Moulay Driss Zerhoun, often referred to simply as Moulay Idriss, is a town located in northern Morocco. It is of great historical and religious significance, as it was founded by Moulay Idriss I, who was the great-grandson of Prophet Muhammad. Moulay Idriss I is considered the founder of the Idrisid dynasty, which ruled over parts of North Africa from the 8th to the 10th centuries.

The town of Moulay Idriss is known as a major pilgrimage site for Moroccan Muslims. The mausoleum of Moulay Idriss I, where his tomb is located, is a focal point for pilgrims and visitors alike. It is considered a sacred place, and non-Muslims are not allowed to enter the mausoleum. Aside from its religious significance, Moulay Idriss Zerhoun is also renowned for its picturesque setting. It is perched on a hill overlooking the surrounding landscape, providing stunning views of the area. The town's traditional architecture, narrow streets, and vibrant marketplaces add to its charm. Visitors can explore the local culture, enjoy traditional Moroccan cuisine, and experience the warm hospitality of the local population. Due to its close proximity to the Roman ruins of Volubilis, a UNESCO World Heritage site, many tourists combine visits to both Moulay Idriss and Volubilis, making it a popular destination for history enthusiasts and travelers interested in exploring Morocco's rich past.
